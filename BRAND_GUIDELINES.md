# Brand Voice & Messaging

## Tone Guidelines

### DO
- Use precise, technical language without jargon theater
- Write short sentences during high-stakes contexts
- Project controlled confidence
- Reference specific metrics and outcomes
- Speak like a practitioner, not a vendor

### DON'T
- Use fear-based marketing ("hackers are everywhere")
- Oversell or make unsubstantiated claims
- Use generic security imagery (locks, hoodies, skulls)
- Speak in vague generalities
- Sound desperate or sales-driven

## Voice Examples

| Context | Bad Example | Good Example |
|---------|-------------|--------------|
| Hero headline | "Protect Your Business from Cyber Threats" | "When the Network Falls Silent, We Restore the Signal" |
| Service description | "We help you with ransomware attacks" | "We've negotiated with every major ransomware syndicate. We know their breaking points." |
| Urgency | "Call us now before it's too late!" | "Every minute increases blast radius." |
| Credentials | "We work with big companies" | "Twenty years defending the institutions that move markets" |

## Key Phrases & Terminology

### Approved Terms
- Zero-Dwell Response
- Agentic AI forensics
- Identity-First containment
- Post-Quantum readiness
- Attack surface
- Lateral movement
- Dwell time
- Blast radius
- Patient zero
- Chain of custody
- Threat actor

### Avoid These Terms
- Hacker (use "threat actor" or "adversary")
- Cyber attack (use "security incident" or "breach")
- Solution (use "capability" or "protocol")
- Best-in-class (meaningless)
- Cutting-edge (overused)
- Synergy (corporate speak)

### Service 01: Rapid Containment & Identity Locking

**Headline:** The Breach Started With a Credential. We End It There.

**Description:**
Modern attacks don't breach firewalls—they compromise identities. A single stolen session token can traverse your entire environment before traditional detection even fires. Our Identity-First Containment protocol treats every authentication event as a potential attack vector.

**Capabilities:**
- Immediate federation of compromised SSO/IdP sessions across Okta, Azure AD, Ping
- Surgical MFA reset sequences that don't disrupt legitimate users
- Service account privilege revocation with dependency mapping
- Conditional access policy hardening in real-time
- Session token invalidation across distributed infrastructure

**Outcome:**
Lateral movement stops. The attacker's foothold becomes an island.

---

### Service 02: AI-Automated Forensics

**Headline:** Reconstructing the Crime Scene at Machine Speed

**Description:**
Traditional forensics takes weeks. Your board wants answers in hours. Our agentic AI systems perform parallel investigation across every data source simultaneously—correlating EDR telemetry, network flows, authentication logs, and cloud audit trails into a coherent attack narrative.

**Capabilities:**
- Complete attack timeline with sub-second precision
- Indicator of Compromise (IOC) extraction and threat intel correlation
- Patient-zero identification with confidence scoring
- Data exfiltration assessment with scope quantification
- Chain-of-custody documentation that holds up in court

**Outcome:**
You brief your executives with facts, not theories. Within hours, not weeks.

---

### Service 03: Ransomware Negotiation & Rollback

**Headline:** When They Have Your Data, We Have Options

**Description:**
Ransomware isn't just a technical problem—it's a business crisis. We've negotiated with every major ransomware syndicate. We know their patterns, their pressure tactics, and their breaking points. More importantly, we know when negotiation is unnecessary because recovery is possible.

**Capabilities:**
- Immediate backup integrity assessment (can we restore without paying?)
- Threat actor profiling (who are we dealing with?)
- Negotiation strategy development (if payment becomes necessary)
- Cryptocurrency transaction handling with regulatory compliance
- Decryptor validation in isolated environments
- Parallel restoration workstreams to minimize downtime

**Outcome:**
Business continuity preserved. Ransom payments avoided when possible, minimized when necessary.

## Imagery Guidelines

### DO Use
- Abstract data visualizations (flowing lines, network graphs)
- Geometric patterns (grids, hexagons, circuits)
- Terminal/code aesthetics (monospace text, command prompts)
- Subtle particle/pulse animations
- Clean iconography (outline style, consistent stroke width)

### DON'T Use
- Lock/padlock icons (overused)
- Hooded hacker figures
- Skull and crossbones
- Generic shield icons
- Stock photos of "security operations centers"
- Binary code waterfalls (Matrix-style)

### Recommended Visual Treatments
1. **Grid overlays** — Subtle background grid suggesting network topology
2. **Pulse animations** — Rippling effects suggesting active monitoring
3. **Scan lines** — Horizontal sweeps suggesting continuous vigilance
4. **Data flow lines** — Curved paths suggesting information movement
5. **Geometric abstractions** — Angular shapes suggesting precision

---

## Animation Guidelines

### Timing
- Micro-interactions: 150-300ms
- State transitions: 300-500ms
- Page transitions: 500-800ms
- Background animations: 4-8s (subtle, non-distracting)

### Easing Functions
- UI elements: `ease-out` or `cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)`
- Background: `linear` (continuous)
- Hover states: `ease-in-out`

### Key Animations

1. **Cyber Pulse** — Expanding rings from grid intersection points
2. **Scan Line** — Horizontal sweep across sections
3. **Status Blink** — Pulsing status indicators
4. **Fade In Up** — Content reveal on scroll
